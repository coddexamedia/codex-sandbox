PART 1 — THE GOLDEN RULES

Before any script or procedure: these five rules govern every single guest interaction at this hotel. They are non-negotiable.

RULE 1 — The guest is always heard first Let the guest finish. Do not interrupt, explain, or justify until they have said everything they need to say. Silence while they speak is not weakness — it is respect. RULE 2 — Acknowledge before you act The guest does not want an instant solution — they want to feel heard. Say 'I understand' and mean it before you say anything else. RULE 3 — Never argue, never justify Even if you believe the guest is wrong — or exaggerating — do not say so. Every argument you win with a guest costs five future guests. There are no winners in a front-desk argument. RULE 4 — Own it, even if you didn't cause it 'That's not my department' is never acceptable. You represent the hotel. Say 'I'm going to sort this for you' — then sort it, or find someone who can. RULE 5 — Every complaint is logged No exceptions. Even if resolved instantly. The Complaint Log is at the front desk. Date, room number, nature of complaint, action taken, your initials. This protects you and helps management fix recurring problems.

PART 2 — THE 6-STEP COMPLAINT PROCEDURE

Follow these steps in order for every complaint, whether at reception, by phone, or reported mid-stay.

1 STEPLISTEN — without interrupting Face the guest. Make eye contact. Put down what you are doing. Let them speak until they are completely finished. Do not touch the keyboard. Do not look at the screen. Listen.
▶ SAY"I'm very sorry to hear that. Thank you for letting us know."
✕ NEVER"That's not normally how it happens." / "Other guests haven't had that issue." / "Are you sure?"
2 STEPACKNOWLEDGE — confirm you understood Repeat back the key issue in your own words. This proves you listened and prevents misunderstandings.
▶ SAY"So if I've understood correctly, [repeat the issue] — is that right?"
✦ DONod. Write it down in front of them — it shows you are taking it seriously.
✕ NEVER"I already know what the problem is." / "Yes, yes, I get it."
3 STEPAPOLOGISE — sincerely, without excuses Apologise for the experience — not for the hotel being terrible, not with caveats. Short, sincere, human.
▶ SAY"I'm really sorry that happened. That's not the experience we want you to have."
▶ SAY"I completely understand why you're frustrated, and I'm sorry."
✕ NEVER"I'm sorry you feel that way." (this is not an apology — guests know it)
✕ NEVER"I'm sorry but..." — never follow an apology with 'but'
4 STEPACT — offer a solution immediately Within 60 seconds of the apology, offer a concrete action. If you can fix it yourself, do it now. If not, tell them exactly what will happen and when.
▶ SAY"Let me sort this for you right now. I'm going to [action] and it will be done by [time]."
▶ SAY"I'm going to get the manager to speak with you — they'll be here in two minutes."
✦ DOAct immediately. Do not say you will 'look into it' and disappear. Update the guest every few minutes if the fix takes time.
✕ NEVER"There's nothing I can do right now." / "You'll need to speak to someone else."
5 STEPFOLLOW UP — close the loop Once the action is complete, return to the guest personally. Confirm the issue is resolved. This one step prevents most negative reviews.
▶ SAY"I just wanted to check — has that been sorted to your satisfaction?"
▶ SAY"Is there anything else I can do for you this evening?"
✦ DOIf the complaint was serious, offer a small goodwill gesture — see Part 4 for authorised gestures.
✕ NEVERAssume the issue is resolved without checking back with the guest directly.
6 STEPLOG — record every complaint Before the end of your shift, ensure the complaint is recorded in full in the Complaint Log, whether resolved or not.
✦ DORecord: date, time, room number, guest name (if known), nature of complaint, action taken, outcome, your name.
